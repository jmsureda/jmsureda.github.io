About Me

I'm a chilean astrophysicist currently doing my PhD in the Institute for Comutational Cosmology at Durham. My research interest are focused in the use of numercial simulations to understand galaxy formation and evolution, in particular for dwarf galaxies. You can find my CV here.
You can come and contact me either in english or spanish.

Research

I'm interested in studying dwarf galaxies to understand about the nature of dark mater through the use of hydrodynamical simulations. In the past I've also studied the possibility of primordial black holes as dark matter candidates, as part of my master thesis. You can see a list of my publications here.

Outreach

Science outreach is our passionate endeavor to make science accessible to all. For this reason, I always try to be involved in different outreach activities aimed at different audiences, including school students. I am always open to participate in new outreach activities. Feel free to contact me about this.
